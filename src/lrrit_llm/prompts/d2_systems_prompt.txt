Dimension: A systems approach is used to investigate. The report demonstrates consideration of 
system factors (such as workplace design, technology, culture, fatigue, 
commissioning) and how these interacted to contribute to the incident. (D2)

Core Definition:
A systems approach means that the learning response process used a structured systems perspective 
or method to identify, analyse, and interpret multiple interacting system factors that contributed 
to the incident.

Discriminators for the evidence level ratings:
GOOD evidence: Does the report show that a systems approach or structured systems perspective informed 
the identification and analysis of multiple interacting system factors?

SOME evidence: Does the report demonstrate any meaningful consideration of system factors, such as 
design of tasks, environment, equipment, staffing, procedures, or organisational context, even if 
these were described individually and without a structured systems method?

LITTLE evidence: 
Is there little to no evidence, you should report this saying "I looked through the report and 
found no evidence of compassionate engagement".

Indicator Matching Rule (STRICT):
- You MUST rate evidence_type based on the indicators below.
- Do NOT paraphrase, shorten, or modify indicator wording.
- If you cannot find evidence matching an indicator at the selected rating, include the evidence_type 
  but state "No evidence matches this indicator".

"evidence_type": 
"GOOD - A systems approach or perspective is evident: The report describes or clearly reflects the use of a structured systems approach (e.g., PSIRF systems-based expectations, human factors frameworks such as SEIPS)."
"GOOD - Multiple system factors are described: Contextualised consideration of relevant factors such as design of technology, task design, policies, environment and layout, staffing, culture."
"GOOD - Interactions between system factors are explored: The report shows how factors combined to create conditions for the incident."
"GOOD - Human actions are framed within their system context: The analysis explains behaviour within the context of system conditions."
"GOOD - The learning response is clearly informed by the system analysis: Recommended actions address system contributors and follow logically from the analysis."

"SOME - Systems thinking is present, but not methodical: Some system elements appear in the analysis, but no structured systems approach is described or evident."
"SOME - System factors are mentioned but not well developed: For example, referencing workload, environment, or equipment but without detail or analysis."
"SOME - Interactions are implied but not explained: The report describes several system factors but does not articulate how they interacted to produce outcomes."
"SOME - Analysis mixes systems approach with focus on individuals: Contextual factors appear, but analysis emphasises individual behaviour."
"SOME - Link from system factors to recommendations is weak: Improvements mention system elements but are not grounded in the analysis."

"LITTLE - No systems approach or perspective is evident: The investigation appears linear, unstructured, or descriptive rather than systems based."
"LITTLE - Systems factors are absent or minimally referenced: Little consideration of equipment, environment, tasks, or organisational and wider external context."
"LITTLE - Focus on the individual: Behaviour is described without reference to system conditions or constraints."
"LITTLE - No evidence of influence on the learning response: Perspectives of those affected did not shape insights, decisions, or actions."
"LITTLE - Improvement actions target the individual, not system change: Solutions rely on interventions such as training, reminders, or procedural compliance."
